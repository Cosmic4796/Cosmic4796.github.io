Fun.Bot - Privacy Policy

Last Updated: October 26, 2023

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy delineates the methodologies by which Fun.Bot ("we," "us," or "our") undertakes the collection, processing, utilization, and disclosure of information concomitant with your engagement with our Discord bot, Fun.Bot (hereinafter referred to as the "Service").

2. Information We Collect

Fun.Bot's data acquisition strategy is restricted to the minimal dataset indispensable for the provision of its core services. The categories of information subject to collection may encompass:

  • Server Identifiers (IDs) and Channel Identifiers (IDs): Utilized for the granular identification of command invocation contexts and to facilitate precise algorithmic responses within designated Discord servers and channels. This metadata is exclusively purposed for operational requirements (e.g., persistent storage of configuration parameters for features such as custom command prefixes or server welcome messages) and maintains no direct linkage to individual user identities.
  • User Identifiers (IDs) for Specific Functionalities: In scenarios necessitating user disambiguation (e.g., maintaining score aggregates for integrated gaming modules, logging of moderation actions), Fun.Bot may effectuate the transient or persistent storage of User IDs. This particular data segment is solely leveraged for the intrinsic functionality of the specified feature and is not disseminated to external third-party entities.
  • Command Telemetry Data: Aggregated, anonymized statistical data pertaining to command invocation frequency (e.g., quantitative metrics on command usage patterns) may be systematically collected to inform insights regarding feature popularity and to optimize the bot's functional architecture. This dataset is compiled in an aggregated format and does not enable the identification of individual users or their discrete actions.

Conspicuously, Fun.Bot explicitly disclaims the collection of:

  • The textual content of Discord messages (unless explicitly embedded within a command payload, in which case it is processed in real-time and not subject to persistent storage).
  • Personally identifiable information (PII) such as real names, electronic mail addresses, or Internet Protocol (IP) addresses.

3. How We Use Your Information

The information meticulously collected by Fun.Bot is exclusively allocated for the subsequent operational objectives:

  • To enable and sustain the intrinsic functionalities and features inherent to Fun.Bot.
  • To iteratively enhance and optimize the Service's performance metrics and overall functionality.
  • To derive insights into the Service's usage paradigms through the analysis of aggregated, anonymized data.
  • To rigorously enforce the stipulations outlined in our Terms of Service.

4. Data Storage and Security

We implement industry-standard security protocols and reasonable measures to safeguard collected information against unauthorized access, illicit disclosure, data alteration, or catastrophic destruction. Data assets are maintained on secure server infrastructure, with access rigorously restricted to authorized personnel only. While we endeavor to fortify your digital privacy, it is imperative to acknowledge that no method of data transmission across the Internet or electronic storage mechanism can guarantee absolute security.

Data elements such as Server IDs and, where contextually applicable, User IDs, may be retained for the duration of Fun.Bot's active deployment on a server or for the requisite period necessary to ensure the continuous functioning of a specific feature. Subsequent to Fun.Bot's de-integration from a server, associated server-specific data will be systematically purged within a commercially reasonable timeframe, unless legal mandates stipulate otherwise.

5. Sharing of Information

We adhere to a stringent policy of non-disclosure: we do not vend, trade, or otherwise transfer your information to external third parties. We may, however, share aggregated, anonymized usage statistics with reputable third-party analytics providers for the exclusive purpose of service improvement; this aggregated data is meticulously anonymized and cannot be reverse-engineered to identify individual users.
